Datamoshing is the gingerly corruption of videos to make them look interesting. It has to do with the movement of the pixels and how you manipulate them through corruption and data compression.
The basic way to think about it is in terms of frames. The .avi codec has 3 types of frames, which are:

	I-Frames: Still images that are the least compressible 
	
	P-Frames: Only track movement of pixels from the previous frame
	
	B-Frames: Take information from the previous and next frames and are the most compressible

B-Frames aren't really somthing to focus on, in fact we don't want any. The bread and butter of datamoshing is I and P frames.
Using an old video editing program called Avidemux (I use 2.5.6) we can encode video so it specifically only have 1 I frame and numerous p frames.
